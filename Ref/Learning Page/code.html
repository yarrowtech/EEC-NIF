Physics • Grade 12

Introduction to Quantum Mechanics

A foundational exploration into the wave-particle duality, uncertainty principles, and the probabilistic nature of the microscopic universe.

target

Learning Objectives

  • 01

    Synthesize the experimental evidence for wave-particle duality, focusing on the double-slit experiment.

  • 02

    Calculate de Broglie wavelengths for various particles and interpret their physical significance.

  • 03

    Explain Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle and its implications for the measurement of momentum and position.

Instructional Flow

The Hook: The Classical Paradox

15 MIN

Present students with the "Ultraviolet Catastrophe." Challenge their understanding of wave physics by showing how classical mechanics fails to explain blackbody radiation. Use a high-definition simulation to visualize the divergence.

Direct Instruction: Quanta & Wave-Duality

30 MIN

Introduce Max Planck’s constant and the concept of discrete energy levels. Use the interactive whiteboard to derive E=hf. Transition into Einstein’s Photoelectric Effect to solidify the concept of light as a particle.

Key Insight

Light isn't 'sometimes' a wave and 'sometimes' a particle; it is a single entity that exhibits different properties depending on the measurement.

Guided Practice: Calculating De Broglie

20 MIN

Students will work in pairs to calculate the wavelength of an electron moving at 10% the speed of light, versus a baseball thrown at 90mph. This emphasizes why quantum effects are only observable at micro-scales.

Synthesis: The Uncertainty Lab

25 MIN

Using the PhET 'Quantum Bound States' simulation, students adjust the potential wells and observe how increasing the precision of position affects the energy (momentum) of the particle.

Assessment

A holistic approach to measuring student mastery through conceptual reasoning and mathematical application.
